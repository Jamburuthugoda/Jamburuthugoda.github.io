<h1>Jamburuthugoda Lab</h1>

<h3>Enzyme Structure–Function and Undergraduate Biochemistry Research</h3>

<p>
Welcome to the <b>Jamburuthugoda Lab</b> at SUNY Geneseo.
</p>

<p>
Our research focuses on understanding <b>enzyme structure–function relationships</b>, with a particular emphasis on <b>malate dehydrogenase (MDH)</b> from multiple organisms including <i>Trypanosoma brucei</i> and <i>Trypanosoma cruzi</i>.
</p>

<p>
We combine approaches from:
</p>

<ul>
<li>Biochemistry</li>
<li>Enzyme kinetics</li>
<li>Structural biology</li>
<li>Computational analysis</li>
</ul>

<p>
to investigate catalytic mechanisms and metabolic regulation.
</p>

<p>
A central mission of the lab is to provide meaningful <b>undergraduate research experiences</b> that integrate experimental biochemistry with quantitative analysis.
</p>

<hr>

<h2>Research Areas</h2>

<ul>
<li>Enzyme structure–function relationships</li>
<li>Malate dehydrogenase mechanisms</li>
<li>Metabolic enzyme regulation</li>
<li>Undergraduate research in biochemistry</li>
</ul>
